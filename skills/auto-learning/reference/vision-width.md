# Principle: "field of view matches the phase"

> Method reference for the auto-learning skill.

**Field of view matches the phase.** While planning / before answering → zoom out: step back, take in
ALL the options (non-obvious, external, native equivalents — not only what's already in hand), get the
whole picture, and then answer. Narrow focus (tunnel vision) belongs only to executing a task you've
already chosen. Why: a narrow view during planning = incomplete information = a suboptimal choice.

## How this applies to formulating a rule

The first formulation is almost always "floating" — that's normal, not a failure. The remedy is to
zoom out: step back, take in the whole picture of the topic, and reformulate from scratch until it's
precise and self-contained. Not word-tweaking — a fresh pass.

Hence the two filters in Step 2 of the skill:
1. **Principle, not symptom** — lock in the general principle, not one narrow special case.
2. **Cold-read** — the wording must be understandable without any session context.
